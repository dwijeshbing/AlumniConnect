Anand Illa

Anand Illa is a seasoned professional currently serving as the Senior Director (SOC Design) at Intel. His academic journey began with a B.Tech from GVP Engineering College, followed by an M.Tech in VLSI Design from IIT Madras, which he completed between 2001 and 2003. These academic accomplishments laid a strong foundation for his future endeavors in the field of semiconductor design and technology.

Anand's career at Intel Corporation spans over 21 years, marked by progressive growth in responsibilities and leadership roles. He started his professional journey at Intel in February 2003 as a Design Automation Engineer. In this role, he was instrumental in driving the physical convergence and TapeIn of a single die, managing IP coordination, and providing critical feedback on IP design quality. His contributions extended to full chip integration and design methodologies, where he worked on developing DFM, layout productivity scripts, and memory design. In April 2005, Anand advanced to the role of Senior Design Engineer, where he played a pivotal role in the Dunnington (45nm Server) project. His responsibilities included full chip integration methodology, custom layout design methodologies, and productivity scripts. He led efforts in DFM, FCL convergence, and Tape-In for the product, which was recognized for having the largest die in the world by transistor count at that time.

Anand's leadership abilities became more pronounced when he took on the role of Engineering Manager in April 2008. Over the next several years, he managed various high-profile projects, including products like Westmere-EX, EP, Core & GT-IP's (Haswell, Ivybridge), and later, Xeon-D and Xeon Broadwell SOCs. His work involved full chip layout convergence, metal fill solutions, and custom layout design methodologies. Anand was also responsible for physical convergence across design styles, efficient derivative development, and enhancing SoC solutions to meet high-frequency needs.

In April 2014, Anand continued as an Engineering Manager, this time focusing on building Intel India's 22FFL Test Chip execution team. He partnered to develop IP-ECO systems for product execution and managed teams responsible for full chip floorplan, structural design, SOC-IP integration, clocking, power delivery, and full chip timing and EDA for server products. His career trajectory took a significant leap in October 2016 when he was promoted to Director (SOC Design). In this role, Anand led SOC SD execution with a laser-sharp focus on schedule, quality, and managing dependencies across all SD implementation domains. He set priorities, tracked progress, and defined processes to ensure the successful implementation of partitions across various teams. Since September 2020, Anand has been serving as the Senior Director (SOC Design) at Intel. In this capacity, he continues to drive SOC SD execution and build partnerships with different business units within Intel, ensuring that the company's execution strategies are aligned across the organization. In addition to his impressive career at Intel, Anand has also ventured into the fintech and agritech sectors as a self-employed professional. Since September 2020, he has been a Micro Investor in fintech, analyzing the ecosystem to identify and support promising startups in Bangalore. Since April 2021, he has also been working as an Analyst in agritech, focusing on AI/ML solutions targeted towards productivity enhancement.

Anand Illa's journey is a testament to his expertise in semiconductor design, his leadership in driving complex projects, and his entrepreneurial spirit in exploring new technological frontiers
